Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Use of unpolished/ unpainted surfaces
  • Elimination of decorative elements (e.g. moldings, print-ons)
  • Use of placed-on instead of integrated elements (e.g. use of clip-on solutions instead of moldings)
  • ...
Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Reduction/elimination of connection types (e.g. USB, Bluetooth, NFC, LTE)
  • Elimination of prerequisites for software updatability (e.g. Function on Demand, OTA updates)
  • Use of previous generation communication electronics/ CPUs
  • ...
Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Reduction/elimination of interfaces (e.g. gesture control, speech recognition)
  • Elimination of compatibility with standardized platforms (e.g. with SAP, Google OS, Siemens S7)
  • Reduction of customizability (e.g. elimination of variants and adjustability)
  • ...
Levers for material costs reduction include:

Levers for material costs reduction include:

Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Reduction of noise, vibration and harshness (NVH) components (e.g. insulation material)
  • Reduction of convenience features (e.g. remote controllability)

Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Elimination of material finishing (e.g. rust prevention, corrosion resistance)
  • Reduction of special purpose materials (e.g. use of hard plastics instead of foamed plastics)
  • Reduction of test requirements/specifications (e.g. lifetime, number of test cycles)
  • ...
Levers for material costs reduction include:
  • Elimination of functions for house repairs (e.g. accessibility of parts via lids)

  • Reduction of remote diagnostics features
